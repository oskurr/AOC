Beginnings

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, often called AOC, is a prominent figure in American politics known for her progressive ideas and activism. She was born on October 13, 1989, in the Bronx, New York City. AOC attended Boston University, where she studied economics and international relations. After graduating , she returned to the Bronx and worked as a bartender and waitress to support her family. During this time, she became increasingly aware of the social and economic issues affecting her community, like poverty and lack of healthcare. Policies

Affordable Housing: AOC advocates for policies that promote affordable housing, aiming to combat gentrification and ensure that residents have access to safe, affordable homes.
Healthcare Access: She supports universal healthcare initiatives, including Medicare for All, to ensure that all residents have access to quality healthcare services.
Green New Deal: AOC champions environmental justice and the Green New Deal, which aims to address climate change while creating jobs and improving infrastructure in low-income communities.
Education: She has pushed for increased funding for public schools, making college more affordable, and addressing student loan debt.
Economic Justice: AOC supports raising the minimum wage and strengthening workers' rights, advocating for policies that help lift people out of poverty.
Immigration Reform: She is an advocate for humane immigration policies and protecting the rights of immigrants, reflecting the diverse population of the Bronx.
Public Safety and Criminal Justice Reform: AOC promotes reforms aimed at reducing police violence and addressing systemic issues within the criminal justice system.

Her Role in The Bronx

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez has really made a difference in the Bronx by standing up for her community. She's fighting for affordable housing so people can afford to live where they grew up, and she's pushing for better environmental conditions since the Bronx faces a lot of pollution. AOC is all about creating jobs and helping local businesses, and she makes sure to connect with residents to hear their concerns. Her work shows how dedicated she is to making the Bronx a better place for everyone!

Her Role in Queens

AOC also fights for comprehensive immigration reform, advocating for pathways to citizenship and protections for immigrant communities. She emphasizes the need for increased funding in public education and works to reduce student debt, making higher education more accessible. Additionally, she supports raising the federal minimum wage and strengthening labor rights, believing in fair working conditions for all. AOC is an advocate for criminal justice reform, aiming to reduce mass incarceration and address racial disparities within the system. Her commitment to social justice extends to policies promoting gender equality and reproductive rights, as well as common-sense gun control measures. She also highlights the importance of food justice, working to improve access to healthy food in underserved areas. Through these efforts, AOC seeks to create a more equitable and sustainable future for all.

Her endorsement for Vice President Kamala Harris

At the Democratic National Convention, Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez passionately endorsed Kamala Harris, stating, “Kamala Harris embodies the hope and resilience of our movement. She has fought tirelessly for justice and equality, and I am proud to support her as we work together to build a brighter future for all Americans.” This endorsement underscored AOC’s commitment to uniting progressives and promoting a diverse leadership in the Democratic Party. "Just because the choice is clear to us, doesn't mean the path will be easy. If you are a working parent trying to afford rent and child care, kamala is for you. If you are an immigrant family just starting your American story, Kamala is for you." she told the Chicago crowd back in August.
